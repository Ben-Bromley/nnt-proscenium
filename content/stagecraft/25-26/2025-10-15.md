# Makeup (Including Fake Injuries)

An introduction to stage makeup techniques, including how to create realistic bruises, cuts, and other injuries for theatrical productions.

This hands-on workshop will teach you both everyday stage makeup and special effects techniques that are essential for many productions.

## What We'll Cover

### Basic Stage Makeup
- **Foundation and Base**: Creating an even skin tone that works under stage lights
- **Contouring**: Enhancing or changing facial structure for character work
- **Eye Makeup**: Techniques that read well from the audience
- **Color Theory**: How colors behave under different lighting conditions

### Special Effects Makeup
- **Bruises**: Creating realistic bruising in various stages of healing
- **Cuts and Wounds**: Using makeup and simple prosthetics for injury effects
- **Aging Techniques**: Adding years to a character's appearance
- **Character Work**: Transforming actors into different characters

### Tools and Materials
- Essential makeup brushes and their uses
- Different types of makeup products (grease paint, cream makeup, powders)
- Special effects materials (wax, fake blood, scar plastic)
- Proper cleaning and maintenance of makeup tools

### Safety and Hygiene
- Preventing cross-contamination and infection
- Allergy considerations and patch testing
- Proper makeup removal techniques
- Storage and care of makeup supplies

## Hands-On Practice

You'll practice creating:
- A basic stage makeup look suitable for general performance
- Realistic bruising using color layering techniques
- Simple cuts or wounds using makeup and basic materials
- Quick character transformation techniques

## What's Provided

- All makeup materials and tools for the workshop
- Mirrors and proper lighting for makeup application
- Makeup removal supplies
- Reference images and guides

## What to Bring

- Hair tie or headband to keep hair away from your face
- Any personal allergies or sensitivities information
- Willingness to experiment and have fun!

Perfect for anyone interested in makeup artistry, character work, or special effects for theatre!
